About the final report, the goal is to submit a report in the format of a conference or a journal. These are some final papers coming from MPCS projects, see them in samples/ folder.

Your report doesn’t need to be as extensive as a full research paper, but it should follow a clear structure. Begin with an introduction that explains the motivation for the problem i.e., why it matters and why it’s challenging. Then include a related-work section summarizing prior approaches and techniques. After that, describe your methodology and how you addressed the problem. Follow with an experiments and results section, where you define the evaluation metrics and compare your approach with existing methods. Finally, conclude with a summary and potential future directions.
If your work is more engineering-focused than methodological, you can still motivate it by explaining the limitations of existing systems, why an improved solution is needed, and how your design compares to what already exists.
