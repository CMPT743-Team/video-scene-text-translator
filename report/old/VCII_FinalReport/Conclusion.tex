\section{Conclusion}

We presented a modular pipeline for cross-language scene text replacement in video that integrates detection, tracking, geometric frontalization, diffusion-based text editing, and appearance-aware propagation.

The key idea of our approach is to decouple the problem into geometry, semantics, and appearance, enabling text editing to be performed once on a reference frame and propagated consistently across time. This design significantly improves temporal stability while preserving geometric accuracy and visual realism.

Experimental results demonstrate that our method outperforms generative video editing baselines in terms of temporal consistency and geometric alignment, while maintaining competitive perceptual quality.

Despite these improvements, challenges such as detection instability, tracking drift, and editing artifacts remain open problems. Future work will explore improved temporal modeling, stronger text generation models, and better handling of occlusions and long video sequences.
